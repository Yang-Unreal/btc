# ⚡️ The Bitcoin Capital Allocation Directive

### Executive Summary
Bitcoin is not a tech stock; it is a **measure of global liquidity**. Its price action is a function of fiat currency abundance relative to its fixed supply. Success requires a "Top-Down" approach: **Macro determines direction; Technicals determine entry.**

---

## 1. The Core Thesis: Liquidity vs. Scarcity
*Do not trade Bitcoin in a vacuum. Understand the engine driving the price.*

The fundamental formula for Bitcoin valuation is:
$$ \text{Price} = \frac{\text{Global Liquidity (M2)}}{\text{Fixed Supply (21M)}} $$

### A. The Macro Indicators (Directional Filter)
Before opening a chart, assess the monetary environment.
*   **Global M2 Supply:** Is the global money supply expanding? **(Primary Driver)**
*   **Fed Balance Sheet:** Expansion (QE) = Buy. Contraction (QT) = Caution.
*   **Real Interest Rates:** Negative Real Rates (Inflation > Yields) drive capital to hard assets.
*   **DXY (USD Index):** Bitcoin acts as an inverse-dollar play. Weak DXY = Strong BTC.

### B. The First Principles
*   **Inelastic Supply:** Unlike gold or real estate, increased demand cannot trigger increased supply. The issuance is fixed by code.
*   **The Halving:** Every 4 years, new supply is cut by 50%. In a flat demand environment, price must rise.

## 2. The Technical Strategy: The "Top-Down" Trinity
*Never trade on a single signal. Use this three-step validation.*

### Step 1: Trend Structure (The "State")
*   **EMA Filter:** Use the Daily/Weekly EMA (e.g., 20/50/200).
    *   Price > EMA = **Long Only** bias.
    *   Price < EMA = **Cash/Short** bias.
*   **Market Structure:** Higher Highs + Higher Lows = Bull Trend.

### Step 2: Momentum (The "Fuel")
*   **RSI Divergence (The High-Probability Signal):**
    *   **Bearish Divergence:** Price makes a New High, RSI makes a Lower High $\rightarrow$ **Sell/Take Profit.**
    *   **Bullish Divergence:** Price makes a New Low, RSI makes a Higher Low $\rightarrow$ **Buy Entry.**

### Step 3: Timing (The "Trigger")
*   **TD Sequential:** Look for "9" or "13" counts to identify trend exhaustion.
*   **Volume:** Price moves must be validated by volume. Low volume breakouts are often fake-outs.

## 3. Risk Management: The Survival Protocols
*You cannot compound capital if you lose it. Preservation > Appreciation.*

### ☠️ The Iron Laws
1.  **Stop Loss:** Mandatory. If you enter a trade without an invalidation point, you are gambling.
2.  **Position Sizing:** Risk max **1-2%** of total portfolio equity per trade.
3.  **Leverage Cap:**
    *   **Beginners:** 1x (Spot only).
    *   **Intermediate:** Max 3x.
    *   **Expert:** Max 10x.
    *   *Note:* Leverage is a speed amplifier, not a win-rate booster.

### Derivative Logic (Shorting/Funding)
*   **The Asymmetry Trap:** Shorting has capped upside (100%) and unlimited downside. It is structurally more dangerous than longing.
*   **Funding Rates:**
    *   **High Positive Rates:** Longs paying Shorts. Signals extreme greed/overcrowding. **Contrarian Sell Signal.**
    *   **Negative Rates:** Shorts paying Longs. Signals extreme fear. **Contrarian Buy Signal.**
